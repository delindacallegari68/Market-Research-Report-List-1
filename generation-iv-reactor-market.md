<p><p>Generation IV reactors represent advanced nuclear technologies aimed at enhancing safety, efficiency, and sustainability. The market types include:</p><p>1. **Very High-Temperature Reactor (VHTR)**: Uses helium as a coolant, ideal for hydrogen production.</p><p>2. **Supercritical Water Reactor (SCWR)**: Operates with water above its critical point, improving efficiency.</p><p>3. **Sodium-cooled Fast Reactor (SFR)**: Employs liquid sodium for efficient heat transfer and fuel recycling.</p><p>4. **Molten Salt Reactor (MSR)**: Utilizes liquid salts as fuel and coolant.</p><p>5. **Lead-cooled Fast Reactor (LFR)**: Employs liquid lead for cooling, enhancing safety.</p><p>6. **Gas-cooled Fast Reactor (GFR)**: Uses gas as a coolant, promoting high thermal efficiency.</p></p>
