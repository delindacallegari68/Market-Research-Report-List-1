<p><p>The green textiles market comprises two main types: degradable and recyclable textiles. Degradable textiles are designed to break down naturally over time, minimizing environmental impact and reducing waste. These materials often include natural fibers or bioplastics that degrade through microbial action. In contrast, recyclable textiles are made from materials that can be reprocessed and transformed into new fabrics after their initial use. This reduces the reliance on virgin resources and promotes a circular economy, aligning with sustainable practices.</p></p>
