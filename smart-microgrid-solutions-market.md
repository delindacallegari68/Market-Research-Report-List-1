<p><p>The Smart Microgrid Solutions market can be categorized into two main types: grid-connected microgrids and off-grid microgrids. Grid-connected microgrids are integrated with larger utility networks, allowing for energy exchange and enhancing reliability while supporting local energy needs. In contrast, off-grid microgrids operate independently from the central grid, making them ideal for remote areas or emergencies where grid access is unavailable. Both types focus on improving energy efficiency, resilience, and sustainability through advanced technologies and energy management systems.</p></p>
