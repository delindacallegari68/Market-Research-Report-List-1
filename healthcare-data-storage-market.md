<p><p>The healthcare data storage market comprises three main types: magnetic storage, flash storage, and solid-state storage. Magnetic storage utilizes traditional hard disk drives (HDDs) to store large volumes of data at lower costs, making it suitable for archival purposes. Flash storage, including USB drives and SSDs, provides faster data access and is ideal for real-time applications, though typically at a higher price point. Solid-state storage combines the speed of flash with enhanced durability, making it a premium choice for critical healthcare data management.</p></p>
