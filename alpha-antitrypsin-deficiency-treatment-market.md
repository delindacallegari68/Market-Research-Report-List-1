<p><p>Alpha-1 Antitrypsin Deficiency (AAT deficiency) is a genetic condition that leads to a deficiency of the protein alpha-1 antitrypsin, which protects the lungs and liver from damage. Treatment primarily involves augmentation therapy, where patients receive intravenous infusions of alpha-1 antitrypsin derived from human plasma to restore normal levels of the protein. Other treatment options may include supportive therapies such as bronchodilators, corticosteroids, and, in severe cases, lung or liver transplantation.</p><p>The Alpha-1 Antitrypsin Deficiency Treatment Market is expected to grow at a CAGR of 7.8% during the forecast period, driven by rising awareness and diagnosis rates of the condition, along with advancements in therapeutic approaches. An increase in the aging population and a higher incidence of related respiratory and liver diseases are also contributing factors. Technological innovations in drug delivery and the development of new recombinant therapies are expected to enhance treatment options further. Moreover, ongoing research into gene therapies holds potential for long-term solutions in managing this deficiency, likely propelling market growth in the coming years.
