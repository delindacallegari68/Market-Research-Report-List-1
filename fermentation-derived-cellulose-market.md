<p><p>The fermentation-derived cellulose market is characterized by three main types: suspension, dispersibility, and dilutes. Suspension refers to cellulose products that remain uniform in a liquid medium, often used for stability in formulations. Dispersibility indicates how well cellulose can be distributed in various solvents, crucial for applications in food, pharmaceuticals, and cosmetics. Dilutes describe cellulose forms that are thinner and more fluid, enabling easy integration into products without altering consistency, enhancing versatility across multiple industries.</p></p>
