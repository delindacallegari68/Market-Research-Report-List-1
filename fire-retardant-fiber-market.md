Phosphorus-based fire retardant fibers integrate phosphorus compounds to enhance flame resistance, making them suitable for textiles and construction materials. Silica-based fire retardant fibers, on the other hand, utilize silica to create a barrier against heat and flames, often found in insulation and protective clothing. Both types are essential for improving safety and compliance in industries such as construction, textiles, and automotive.</p></p>
