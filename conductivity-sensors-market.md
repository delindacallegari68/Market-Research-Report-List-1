<p><p>The conductivity sensors market includes various types such as inductive and electrodeless conductivity sensors. Inductive conductivity sensors operate using electromagnetic fields to measure the conductivity of fluids without direct contact, making them ideal for corrosive or dirty environments. Electrodeless conductivity sensors utilize non-contact methods, often relying on capacitance principles, to gauge the conductivity of solutions. Both types offer high accuracy and reliability, catering to diverse applications in industries such as water treatment, chemical processing, and pharmaceuticals.</p></p>
